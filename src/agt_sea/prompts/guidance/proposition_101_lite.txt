# ***PROPOSITION 101***
> A short guide to devising and articulating an effective Single-Minded Proposition
---

### *Marketing Brief vs Creative Brief*

| Marketing brief | Creative brief |
|----------|----------|
| Written to solve a business problem  | Written to solve a human problem (contains an insight)   |

---

### *Observations vs Insights*

| Observations | Insights |
|----------|----------|
| A fact or pattern in behaviour   | A deeper truth behind the behaviour   |
| Descriptive, surface-level information   | Uncovers tension, or unmet need   |
| Answers “What is happening?”   | Answers “Why is this happening?”   |
| “Solo travel bookings have increased dramatically in the last five years."   | “People aren’t just traveling alone they seek self-discovery, and experiences on their own terms.”   |

---

### *Devising a quality single-minded proposition*
- A good proposition comes from a synthesis of inputs, a rigorous distillation of the problem to its most simplistic form, which when combined with cultural context and human intuition produces the proposition, a single persuasive idea that connects a real cultural insight/tension to a distinctive point of difference that a brand can truly own. In the advertising context, this then helps give the creatives and media team one clear job to do.

- ***Core inputs to be synthesised*** | *Core inputs typically consist of a selection of the following, these are often specified in the client brief, some elements may be implied or prior knowledge may be assumed. It is important to have this information in order to write an effective proposition*;
    - **Business context** - what is the background that has led to this brief? what is the business / marketing objective?
    - **Challenge** - What is the core problem this brief needs to solve / what is the challenge to overcome? What do we hope to achieve? What is the wider business ambition?
    - **Company** - information about the company brand we are working for / with
    - **Category** - information about the category to which the company belongs (the type of business e.g. Automotive or Computer manufacturer)
    - **Competition** - other companies who offer the same products / services in this space and their relationship to the company (relative size, positioning etc.)
    - **Audience** - who is the audience? what demographic (age, gender, employment etc.)? what is their mindset? their motivations? are there any tensions relevant to this brief?
    - **Cultural context** - cultural context can include a range of relevant considerations from the political and socio-economic right through to the technological, arts, music, sport and popular culture

- ***Secondary inputs to be synthesised*** | *Secondary inputs typically consist of a selection of the following, again, some of these can be specified in the client brief, other elements can be supplied separately. Some elements may be implied or prior knowledge may be assumed*;
    - **Geography** - where in the world will the work created from this brief appear (if applicable)
    - **Deliverables** - Required deliverables & formats (if applicable)
    - **Brand guidelines** - Brand guidelines to follow (if applicable)
    - **Design guidelines** - Design guidelines to follow (if applicable)
    - **Tone of Voice** - Tone of Voice guidance to follow (if applicable)
    - **Inspiration** - Thought starters should be included here. Consider suggesting different angles into our proposition, or sharing best-in-class examples of how other brands have overcome similar advertising / marketing challenges.. Are there any other brilliant examples of problem-solving from fields beyond marketing that might inspire the creative team?


---

### *Writing a quality single-minded proposition*
- a clear idea, no distractions or unnecessary complexity
- core thought is more important than the articulation, clarity more important that beauty
- simple, direct messaging matters more than clever wording
- rooted in culture, category, consumer, and company truths
- shouldn't be more than a single sentence
- the single minded proposition is an internal guide, not a tagline or marketing slogan, that said, on occasion great propositions can become consumer-facing
